In case you select "Q mean (selected period)" , "Q min (selected period)", "Q max (selected period)":

  • The plot in this panel shows the percentiles of streamflow value (mean, min, or max) during the selected period compared to the streamflow value (mean, min, or max) of all other years (1980 to current year) for the same period.
  • So if you select streamflow value "Q mean (selected period)" , "Q min (selected period)", "Q max (selected period)" the mean, min, and max value will be used, respectively.
  • The period considered is the one you defined in the "Select period" option.
  • The coloring scheme is adapted from the USGS WaterWatch
    • Highest: Highest value during the entire time
    • Above 90th percentile: Much above normal
    • 75th - 90th percentile: Above normal
    • 25th - 75th percentiles: Normal
    • 10th - 25th percentile: Below normal
    • Below 10th percentile: Much below normal
    • Lowest: Lowest value during the entire time

In case you select "Q_last_day":

  • The plot in this panel shows the percentiles of streamflow of the last of day in the base data compared to all streamflow values.
  • The coloring scheme incase of Q percentile classes = "All" is the same as above.
  • In case of Q percentil classes = "Flood", the percentile classes are (USGS WaterWatch):
    • Above 99th percentile: Extreme hydrologic flood
    • 95th - 99th percentile: Servere hydrologic flood
    • Below 95th percentile: No flood
  • In case of Q percentil classes = "Drought", the percentile classes are (USGS WaterWatch):
    • Above 25th percentile: No drought
    • 10th - 25th percentile: Below normal
    • 5th - 10th percentile: Moderate hydrologic drought
    • Below 5th percentile: Servere hydrologic drought
    • Lowest: Extreme hydrologic drought
